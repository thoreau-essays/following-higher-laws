In this essay, we discuss Thoreau's revisions to the chapter titled "Higher Laws" in *Walden* as published in 1854. For evidence, we draw on the manuscript of *Walden* in the Huntington Library, HM 924.

The manuscript of *Walden* passed through a number of hands before it came to the Huntington in 1918. One person who had access to the manuscript was Thoreau admirer Franklin Benjamin Sanborn, who marked it up in various ways. On the manuscript pages discussed in this essay, page numbers in the left and right margins, and the notation "Much re-written" at the top of two pages, are in Sanborn's hand. Page numbers in the upper right corner, however, are Thoreau's; in his first draft of *Walden*, he numbered pages 1–51 in "Economy" and pages 1–235 elsewhere in the material. Often, he numbered only every other recto. His numbering, together with gaps in the text, constitute evidence that some leaves in the sequence are missing.{% include essay/feature/aside.html text="On Thoreau's pagination, see J.
